Executive Summary

Geek Protocol is infrastructure for learning in Web3.

While most decentralized systems reward capital and speculation, Geek Protocol rewards demonstrated knowledge through skill-based participation.

Instead of credentials, users provide proof. Instead of grinding, users compete. Instead of gatekeepers, rewards are transparent and on-chain.

Geek Protocol begins as a Quiz-to-Earn experience and evolves into a public Proof-of-Learning layer for culture, education, and community.

The Problem

Education

  • Credentials are slow, expensive, and centralized
  • Learning outcomes are opaque
  • Students are treated as passive

Web3

  • “Earn” models reward time or money, not skill
  • Automation and farming dominate
  • Cultural knowledge has no value layer

Knowledge exists — but it isn’t rewarded.

Proof-of-Learning

Geek Protocol introduces a new on-chain primitive: Proof-of-Learning.

  • Knowledge is demonstrated, not claimed
  • Skill determines rewards
  • Participation is open and wallet-gated
  • Outcomes are verifiable and repeatable

Learning becomes an active, measurable economic action.

Why Kaspa

Geek Protocol requires fast confirmations, low latency, and predictable fees.

Kaspa enables real-time, user-facing learning experiences without sacrificing decentralization — making it uniquely suited for Proof-of-Learning infrastructure.

How It Works

Learn → Compete → Earn → Progress

  1. Connect a Kaspa wallet
  2. Enter a skill-based quiz session
  3. Answer under time pressure
  4. Earn $GEEK based on performance
  5. Progress through XP, achievements, and collectibles
No pay-to-win No farming No randomness

$GEEK is the coordination layer of the protocol.
