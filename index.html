Executive Summary

Geek Protocol is infrastructure for learning in Web3.

While most decentralized systems reward capital and speculation, Geek Protocol rewards demonstrated knowledge.

The protocol introduces Proof-of-Learning — a system where skill is verified through performance and rewarded transparently on-chain.

Geek Protocol begins as a Quiz-to-Earn experience and evolves into a public learning layer for culture, education, and community.

The Problem

Education

  • Credentials are slow, expensive, and centralized
  • Learning outcomes are opaque
  • Students are treated as passive

Web3

  • “Earn” models reward time or capital, not skill
  • Automation and farming dominate
  • Cultural knowledge has no value layer

Knowledge exists — but it isn’t rewarded.

Proof-of-Learning

Geek Protocol introduces a new on-chain primitive: Proof-of-Learning.

  • Knowledge is demonstrated, not claimed
  • Skill determines rewards
  • Participation is open and wallet-gated
  • Outcomes are verifiable and repeatable

Learning becomes an active, measurable economic action.

Why Kaspa

Geek Protocol requires fast confirmations, low latency, and predictable fees to support real-time, competitive learning.

Kaspa enables user-facing Proof-of-Learning without sacrificing decentralization.

How It Works

Learn → Compete → Earn → Progress

  1. Connect a Kaspa wallet
  2. Enter a skill-based quiz session
  3. Answer under time pressure
  4. Earn $GEEK based on performance
  5. Progress through XP, achievements, and collectibles
No pay-to-win No farming No randomness

Why the $GEEK Token Exists

Proof-of-Learning cannot function without a native on-chain unit of value.

  • Standard: KRC-20
  • Network: Kaspa
  • Supply: 144B fixed
  • Emissions: Performance-based
  • Economics: Recycle & Burn (70% recycle / 30% burn)

Without $GEEK, learning outcomes could not be verified, rewarded, or governed on-chain.

Fairness & Integrity

  • Wallet-gated participation
  • Server-side validation
  • Anti-bot controls
  • No capital advantages

Skill decides outcomes — not leverage.
